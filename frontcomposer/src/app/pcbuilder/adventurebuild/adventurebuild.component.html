A. Open the CPU bracket on your motherboard by pushing on the metal retention arm. Do not remove black cover.

B. Align the arrows on the CPU and the socket. Do not force. Place the CPU down gently, taking care not to move it once seated.

C. Pull down and latch the metal retention arm onto the socket.

D. The plastic CPU cover should come off itself once the bracket is closed. Do not discard this cover in case you need to return or exchange it.
A. Unlatch the CPU socket by pressing on the metal retention arm and bringing it up all the way.

B. Hold the CPU by the sides. Do NOT touch the gold pins. Identify the corner of the CPU that has the arrow.

C. Align the marked corner (dot or triangle) on the CPU with the marked corner of the socket. Do not force or move. Place gently.

D. Pull down and latch the metal retention arm onto the socket.
After securing the CPU into the socket, you see a box appeared from the ground, inside the box there are two rectangle artifacts.
You flip through your notes, and found out that these are called as 'RAM sticks' or 'Memory sticks'. They serve as the short term memory for the computer.
You noticed that there are 4 (some times 2) rectangle slots next to the CPU socket with tabs on the top and bottom of it.
They seem to align with the Memory Sticks as they have a space between a long and short golden line.
Inside your notes (Motherboard manual) you see that you have to install into very specific slots first.
A. Your motherboard may only have tabs on one side to push down.

B.Typically if there is a white sticker on your memory sticks, this white sticker will be facing into the board, towards where the CPU socket is located.

C. Make sure the RAM matches the openings on the motherboard lanes. Otherwise, it will not go in.

D. Make sure that the gold connectors are properly aligned before applying force. Press down until you hear a click. Be careful, but don't be afraid to push firmly, it may require some extra force.

Upon inspection on the motherboard, theres a slot for this artifact, and it is located near the CPU socket.
A. Locate the M.2 slot on your motherboard. It is usually located near the CPU socket.
B. Remove the screws from the M.2 slot cover.
Some boards the screws are separated in the motherboard box.
Set the screws aside in a safe place.
D. Insert the M.2 NVME SSD into the slot at a 30-degree angle. Do not force it in.
E. Push down and secure the M.2 NVME SSD with the screws you set aside.

Before installing any of the components into the case, we would need to first prepare the case for installation.
A. Remove the side panels of the case by unscrewing the screws on the back of the case.
B. You would see a box that contains the screws for the motherboard standoffs and cables for the case.
There will be a box that sits inside a hard drive cage or a compartment in the case.
Inside this box contains all the motherboard standoffs that would be used for the motherboard installation.
There will also be a bag of screws that would be used for the installation of the power supply and the storage drives.
[Box content would vary depending on case chosen.]
A. Lay the case on its side with the transparent panel side up.

B. Check if the motherboard has a IO Shield, its either pre-installed or you would have to install it yourself.

C. If you have a separated IO Shield, install it into the back of the case.

D. Bend the tabs that are on the IO Shield to allow clearances for your motherboard ports.

[Do not bend the tabs all the way. Ensure that they are bent enough to allow the motherboard ports to be inserted.]
Ensure that your motherboard's standoffs are installed in the case and in the correct holes.
You can check by hovering the motherboard above the standoffs and aligning with the standoffs.
If the holes align with the standoffs, then you are good to go.
If you are missing some, install them in the correct holes.
If a standoff is under any part of your motherboard, please remove it or realocate it to the correct hole.
E. Install the motherboard into the case by aligning the ports with the IO Shield and standoffs.

F. Secure the motherboard with the screws that came with the case.

[Ensure that the motherboard is secured and does not move when you shake the case.]

After installing the motherboard, you continue your journey and
enter the next room which has the power supply artifact.
The power supply is a black box with a fan on top or bottom of it with cables that come out of it.

A. Non modular power supplies have all the cables attached to it.
B. Semi modular power supplies have some cables attached to it with connectors for additional cables.
C. Fully modular power supplies have no cables attached to it, all cables are separated and can be attached as needed.
For the purpose of this adventure, we will be installing a non-modular power supply.
A. Place the power supply into the case with the fan facing down.
B. Secure the power supply with the screws that came with the case.

Before installing the GPU, Lets make sure that you have the necessary cables to power the GPU.
A. Locate the PCIe power cables that came with the power supply.
B. Depending on the GPU, you may need a 6-pin, 8-pin, or 6+2 pin PCIe power cable.
On your motherboard, there should be a slot with a latch on the right side of the slot under the CPU socket.
This is the PCIe slot where the GPU will be installed.
A. & B. Remove the PCIe slot covers from the case. If your case has a backplate, remove it for now.

C. Open the latch on the PCIe slot by pushing dow on the latch.

D. & E. Align the GPU with the PCIe slot and the holes in the PCIe slot clovers and push down until you hear a click.

F. Secure the GPU with the screws that came with the case and reinstall the backplate as well.
Finally connect the PCIe power cables to the GPU.
A. Ensure that each hook on the rear side panels are secured and aligned with the case.
B. Slide or swing the side panels back into the case. Ensure that there are no wires in the way.
C. Secure the side panels with the screws that you have removed earlier.
A. Slide the front side panel back into the case. Be careful if your front side panel is made of glass!
B. Secure the front side panel with the screws that you have removed earlier.
A. Connect the power cable to the power supply and the wall outlet.
B. Turn on the power supply switch and press the power button on the case.
C. If you see the BIOS screen, congratulations! You have successfully built your computer.
D. If you do not see the BIOS screen, please check the connections and ensure that all components are properly installed.
