Welcome to the learning page!

A new challenge has appeared! You've discovered the ancient ruins of a lost civilization.
Inside these ruins, you've unearthed functioning Computer parts.
Your mission — should you choose to accept it — is to travel through, collecting these essential parts and assembling them into a working gaming PC!
This guide will help you on your quest.
You have reached the enterance of the Ancient City of Technologica!
The enterance of the Ancient City was covered in vines and a thick forest.
As you entered the ruins, you noticed that there are some scribings on the walls. It was translated to the following:
The city of Technologica was once ruled by two factions, they were 'AMD' and 'Intel'.
They each control vast territories, resources, and armies of loyalists.
AMD's faction was portrayed as the innovative rebels, constantly pushing the boundaries of technology and advocating for open-source principles.
Meanwhile, Intel's faction represents the established order, focusing on stability, security, and efficiency.
But, due to the greed of power and control, both factions were at war for decades, until both nations have drove the city into a complete wasteland.
After reading the scribings, you arrived at a crossroads. On the left, a pulsing red panel with AMD, on the right, a pulsing blue panel with Intel.
Which faction shall you side with?
(If you chose AMD, please choose the panel with the title AMD)
(If you chose Intel, please choose the panel with the title Intel.)
You have chosen to join the rebels to fight against the oppresive force of Intel.
As you walk through the corridor, you reached a huge room that is bathed in a soft, pulsating red glow.
In the middle of the room, you see a Ryzen CPU artifact sits on a pedestal, the glow was resonating with your heartbeat in harmony.
As you approached the artifact, you can feel a surge of creative energy, as if the very essence of innovation and possibility flows through you.
You picked up the artifact and the room's walls awere adorned with holographic projections showcasing AMD's technological innovations and the diverse cultures it had supported.
A door in front of you opens, red lights brighten up the corridor, as a sign for your next step.
You have chosen stand with justice to ensure order against the rebellion of AMD.
As you walk through the corridor, you reached a huge room that is bathed in a steady, blue glow.
In the middle of the room, you see a Core CPU artifact sits on a pedestal, the glow dominated the space with a futuristic blue light.
As you approached the artifact, you can feel a surge ofof order and precision, its presence evoking feelings of confidence and security.
You picked up the artifact and the room's walls, covered with monitors displaying Intel's advanced infrastructure and meticulously planned strategies for dominance.
A door in front of you opens, blue lights brighten up the corridor, as a sign for your next step.
After choosing your faction, you received a map to continue your adventure.
You picked up the map and ventured forth to the doors that were opened.
You follow the lights in the corridor that lead you to a very spacious room.
In the room, you see another artifact that is placed on a pedestal. Theres a text on the pedestal that reads, The Motherboard.
As you apporach the motherboard, you see that in the middle it has a socket which aligns with the CPU you have chosen.
(If you chose AMD, please choose the panel with the title AMD)
(If you chose Intel, please choose the panel with the title Intel.)
A. Open the CPU bracket on your motherboard by pushing on the metal retention arm. Do not remove black cover.

B. Align the arrows on the CPU and the socket. Do not force. Place the CPU down gently, taking care not to move it once seated.

C. Pull down and latch the metal retention arm onto the socket.

D. The plastic CPU cover should come off itself once the bracket is closed. Do not discard this cover in case you need to return or exchange it.
A. Unlatch the CPU socket by pressing on the metal retention arm and bringing it up all the way.

B. Hold the CPU by the sides. Do NOT touch the gold pins. Identify the corner of the CPU that has the arrow.

C. Align the marked corner (dot or triangle) on the CPU with the marked corner of the socket. Do not force or move. Place gently.

D. Pull down and latch the metal retention arm onto the socket.
After securing the CPU into the socket, you see a box appeared from the ground, inside the box there are two rectangle artifacts.
You flip through your notes, and found out that these are called as 'RAM sticks' or 'Memory sticks'. They serve as the short term memory for the computer.
You noticed that there are 4 (some times 2) rectangle slots next to the CPU socket with tabs on the top and bottom of it.
They seem to align with the Memory Sticks as they have a space between a long and short golden line.
Inside your notes (Motherboard manual) you see that you have to install into very specific slots first.
C. Your motherboard may only have tabs on one side to push down.

D.Typically if there is a white sticker on your memory sticks, this white sticker will be facing into the board, towards where the CPU socket is located.

E. Make sure the RAM matches the openings on the motherboard lanes. Otherwise, it will not go in.

F. Make sure that the gold connectors are properly aligned before applying force. Press down until you hear a click. Be careful, but don't be afraid to push firmly, it may require some extra force.
